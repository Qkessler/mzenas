# Linvo — Investment Proposal 2026

## Executive Summary

Every day, millions of people in major cities face the same question: *where should we go tonight?* They scroll through cluttered Google results, juggle review sites, and often default to the same fast-food chains out of sheer decision fatigue. Meanwhile, independent restaurants — the ones with the most original food and authentic experiences — struggle to fill seats, buried under the marketing budgets of large franchises.

Linvo is a platform built on a simple symbiosis: restaurants want operational efficiency, and consumers want their leisure time to be productive. We serve both sides through two interconnected pillars:

1. **An AI-powered operations platform for restaurants** that eliminates friction from reservations through payment, reducing staffing costs and increasing table turnover.
2. **A consumer-facing discovery network** that aggregates real-time dining offers and uses AI-driven personalization to recommend the right restaurant at the right moment.

Our vision is to become the operating system for urban dining — a single platform that manages the entire journey from "should we go out tonight?" to settling the bill. We start by proving value where it matters most: inside the restaurant.

## The Problem

Independent hospitality businesses in major cities face three compounding pressures:

1. **Rising staff costs** — labor is the single largest expense, and hiring reliable front-of-house staff is increasingly difficult.
2. **Market share absorbed by large chains** — franchises like 100 Montaditos, McDonald's, or Tagliatella dominate visibility and foot traffic through sheer brand recognition and marketing spend.
3. **Local competition for attention** — even among independents, standing out on social media or search engines requires time and money most small operators simply don't have.

The result: original gastrobars, gourmet cafes, and neighborhood restaurants remain invisible to most consumers, especially younger demographics who default to what's familiar.

On the consumer side, the experience is equally broken. Searching "tapas restaurants in [city]" on Google returns an overwhelming, outdated list that tells you very little about what's actually happening right now — which places have open tables, which ones are running specials, or which one fits your mood tonight. For tourists, the problem is even worse.

We observe this pattern today in cities like Cartagena and Murcia, and it scales to every major urban center in Spain and beyond. The post-pandemic appetite for going out is real — people want to explore, but the tools to do so efficiently don't exist yet.

## Solution

Linvo is built on two interconnected pillars, deployed in sequence to validate demand before scaling.

### Pillar 1: Restaurant Operations Platform (MVP)

Our first priority is proving that restaurants will adopt and benefit from in-venue software. This is where we start — and where we become sustainable.

The platform provides restaurants with:

- **Reservation management** — real-time table occupancy tracking and forecasting of expected covers days in advance.
- **QR-based digital ordering** — when a customer sits down, they scan a QR code at the table. This locks the table in the system and opens the restaurant's fully customizable digital menu. The customer places their order as they would on a delivery app, but in person. The order goes straight to the kitchen.
- **Integrated payment** — when the customer is ready to leave, they tap "pay" in the app, choose their payment method, and the table is freed for the next party.

The efficiency gain for the business is concrete: waitstaff no longer take orders or process payments — they focus entirely on food service. This reduces the number of front-of-house staff needed, speeds up table turnover, and improves the customer experience through faster, frictionless service.

At its core, every interaction through the platform feeds an AI-driven 360-degree customer profile. Over time, we will know what each customer prefers, how they behave, and what recommendations will resonate — turning transactional data into a personalization engine.

### Pillar 2: Consumer Discovery Network

Once we have a critical mass of restaurants on the platform, we activate the consumer-facing side: a network that aggregates the entire dining and leisure offer of a city into a single, intelligent interface.

Consumers will be able to:

- **Browse and filter** restaurants by cuisine, location, price range, and real-time availability.
- **Reserve tables** directly through the platform.
- **Access "Hot Moments"** — time-limited offers that restaurants push to attract customers during slow periods (e.g., happy hours, last-minute deals, off-peak discounts).

As our dataset grows, we will evolve from a static directory into an **AI-powered recommendation engine** — potentially a conversational interface that suggests where to go based on the customer's history, current offers, time of day, and preferences. Think of it as a personal concierge for urban dining.

This network effect is the real moat: the more restaurants participate, the better the recommendations; the better the recommendations, the more consumers engage; the more consumers engage, the more restaurants want in.

## Value Proposition

Linvo delivers distinct value to each side of the marketplace:

**For restaurants:**
- Reduced staffing requirements — ordering and payment are fully automated, freeing waitstaff to focus on service.
- Increased visibility — independent restaurants gain the same discoverability as large chains, leveling the playing field.
- Operational intelligence — reservation forecasting, real-time occupancy data, and customer behavior analytics.
- Higher table turnover — faster order-to-payment cycles mean more covers per shift.

**For consumers:**
- A single platform to discover, reserve, order, and pay — from "should we go out?" to settling the bill.
- AI-personalized recommendations that improve with every interaction.
- Real-time offers and availability, eliminating guesswork.
- Access to restaurants they would never have found through Google or social media.

What makes Linvo unique is the integration of both sides into one system. Competitors either serve the restaurant (POS software) or the consumer (discovery portals) — we do both, and the data flywheel between them is what creates compounding value.

## Market Opportunity

Our primary target demographic is young adults (22-40) with disposable income, digitally native, living in large urban centers where the dining offer is vast but hard to navigate. In Spain alone, there are over 300,000 hospitality businesses and 47 million residents, with dining out deeply embedded in the culture.

The initial beachhead is mid-sized Spanish cities (Murcia, Cartagena, Valencia, Alicante) where the density of independent restaurants is high but digital adoption among them is low. This allows us to build a concentrated network quickly before expanding to larger markets (Madrid, Barcelona) and eventually other European cities.

The broader trend supports us: digital ordering adoption accelerated during the pandemic and shows no sign of reverting. Consumers increasingly expect the convenience of app-based experiences in physical venues, and restaurants that don't adapt will fall behind.

## Business Model

We adopt a growth mindset: deliver value fast, then build sustainable revenue. Our monetization strategy has two tiers aligned with our product pillars:

**Tier 1 — Restaurant SaaS (primary revenue)**
- Monthly subscription for access to the full operations platform (ordering, reservations, payments, analytics).
- A "light" free tier for restaurants that contribute data to the network in exchange for limited platform access. This accelerates network growth while building the dataset that powers our AI recommendations.

**Tier 2 — Marketplace revenue**
- Commission per reservation or per transaction processed through the platform, similar to TheFork's model.
- Promoted placement and sponsored "Hot Moments" for restaurants that want premium visibility to consumers.

We will not charge consumers directly. The value flows to restaurants through efficiency gains and increased foot traffic — they are the ones who benefit economically and should bear the platform cost.

## Competitive Landscape

Our competitors fall into two distinct categories:

**Restaurant operations software** — POS systems, digital ordering, and table management tools (e.g., Lightspeed, Toast, Covermanager). These solve the in-venue problem but do nothing for consumer discovery. They are potential integration partners or acquisition targets, not direct threats to our full-stack vision.

**Leisure and discovery portals** — TheFork (restaurant reservations in 20+ countries, commission-per-booking model), Fever (event and experience discovery), TripAdvisor, and Google Maps. These serve the consumer side but offer no operational value to restaurants. TheFork is our closest direct competitor: they have scale and experience, but their product stops at the reservation — they don't touch the in-venue experience.

Linvo's differentiation is the bridge between these two categories. No current player owns the full journey from discovery to payment while building an AI-driven profile of the customer across both sides. This integrated approach is our competitive advantage and our defensibility thesis.

## Go-to-Market Strategy

**Phase 1 — Validate (Months 1-6)**
Onboard 20-30 independent restaurants in Murcia and Cartagena onto the operations platform. Prove adoption, measure efficiency gains, and gather early data. Focus exclusively on the B2B side.

**Phase 2 — Launch consumer network (Months 6-12)**
With a critical mass of restaurants, launch the consumer app in the same cities. Drive initial adoption through "Hot Moments" offers and local marketing partnerships.

**Phase 3 — Expand and iterate (Year 2)**
Scale to Valencia, Alicante, and other mid-sized Spanish cities. Introduce AI-powered recommendations. Begin building the conversational recommendation interface.

**Phase 4 — Scale (Year 3+)**
Enter Madrid and Barcelona. Explore adjacent verticals (events, nightlife, food trucks). Evaluate international expansion.

## Open Questions & Next Steps

The following areas require further brainstorming and validation before the proposal is finalized:

1. **AI integration depth** — How deeply do we embed AI into the consumer experience from day one? A static directory is the starting point, but the long-term vision includes a conversational AI concierge. We need to define the roadmap from one to the other.
2. **Data collection from non-paying restaurants** — Can we aggregate public information from restaurants that don't subscribe to the platform, enriching the consumer network even before they convert to paying customers?
3. **App vs. Web vs. Both** — What is the right channel strategy for the consumer-facing product at launch?
4. **Monetization model validation** — The SaaS subscription + marketplace commission model needs to be stress-tested with real restaurant operators before we commit.
