# Investor Dossier

*Leisure dining, reinvented — 2026 investment proposal*

Mzenas is a SaaS platform that digitises and automates the full service cycle in hospitality, from the moment a customer sits down to the moment they pay. The whole system runs on any device with a browser, requires no extra hardware, no complex installation, and no training. A bar can be operational in under five minutes.

The current focus is the B2B software for restaurants: an AI-powered QR menu, automatic orders to the kitchen, table management, and integrated payment. The product is built and tested. The medium-term vision layers a consumer discovery platform on top of that restaurant network, connecting diners with the best restaurants in their city through AI and real-time offers.

The market is saturated with complex tools designed for large chains. Eight out of ten independent bars in Spain don't use them because they can't. That is the opening Mzenas targets. We don't compete on feature count — we compete on simplicity. Simplicity is the product's backbone: simplicity in onboarding, in daily use, and in adoption. No other player in the market offers this.

## The problem we solve

### For the operator

Independent restaurants make up roughly 80% of the Spanish hospitality market. They face constant pressure from labour costs, large chains, and digital invisibility. The tech solutions available today — mandatory hardware, long setup, steep learning curves — were built for a different kind of business. The independent operator is left out.

The consequences are predictable: handwritten tickets, frequent order errors, tables that turn over slowly, and an average ticket below its real potential. The technology to fix this exists, but nobody has made it simple enough.

### For the diner

The urban consumer faces an enormous, disorganised dining offer. Google searches are generic and outdated. Real-time offers are invisible. Tourists don't know where to start. Chains with larger marketing budgets always win the visibility battle, regardless of quality.

AI is reshaping how people discover and choose where to eat. A system that learns the user's preferences, cross-references them with the real and up-to-date offer of their city, and makes accurate recommendations in real time — including offers active in that moment — is the biggest innovation opportunity the sector has seen in a decade.

Mzenas solves both sides. First, it gives the operator a tool that works from day one. Then it connects that operator with the customer who is already looking for them.

## How the product works

### Pillar 1 — Restaurant software (current focus)

The operator uploads their menu in any format — PDF, photo, Excel — and Mzenas's AI extracts dishes, prices, categories, and descriptions automatically. The digital menu is ready on the spot. The system then generates a unique QR code per table, printable in seconds. The diner scans it, browses the menu, places an order, and the order goes straight to the kitchen.

Waitstaff stop taking orders. Their job becomes serving and attending. The table clears automatically when the customer pays from their phone. The whole system runs in any browser — phone, tablet, or monitor — so the POS, kitchen display, and table management all live on the tablet the restaurant already owns. There is no hardware investment.

### Pillar 2 — Discovery platform (medium-term vision)

On top of the restaurant network the software builds, Mzenas will layer a consumer discovery experience: an AI-powered dining catalogue that learns the user's preferences and recommends restaurants, offers, and unique moments in real time.

The key feature of this phase is **Hot Moments** — time-limited offers restaurants push during slow periods. The diner sees, in real time, what is happening in their city right now. The restaurant fills tables that would otherwise stay empty.

The power of this platform lives in what Mzenas knows about the user. Every visit, every order, every dish, every ticket feeds the customer profile. Over time, Mzenas understands a diner's tastes better than any other platform, because no other platform has access to the data generated inside the restaurant. The Fork doesn't. Mzenas does, from day one.

## Value proposition

For the restaurant, Mzenas lowers staffing costs by removing order-taking from the waiter's job, increases table turnover by shortening the order-to-payment cycle, and raises the average ticket through upselling built into the digital menu. Deployment is immediate — under five minutes, no hardware — and the restaurant gains visibility through the Mzenas discovery network from day one.

For the diner, the value is simpler: order from your phone without waiting for a waiter and without downloading any app, discover new restaurants through AI-personalised recommendations, and see real-time offers (Hot Moments) before deciding where to go.

## Market and opportunity

Spain has the highest density of bars and restaurants per capita in the EU — over 270,000 venues, the vast majority of them independent and operationally undigitised. The European hospitality software market grows at double-digit rates each year, driven by operational pressure and the acceleration of contactless payments.

The current offer is polarised: complex tools for large chains on one side, and digital-menu-only products that don't solve the full service cycle on the other. Mzenas's initial target is the independent bar or restaurant with 5 to 30 tables — a huge, under-served segment with a real and urgent need.

## Business model

### Phase 1 — Restaurant SaaS

Monthly subscription per restaurant. Launch price is **€30/month**, deliberately priced below direct competitors to maximise adoption speed. A freemium period is on the table to validate the product with the first cohort of customers.

The model has high margin and low marginal cost: once the product exists, scaling from 100 to 1,000 restaurants does not scale costs proportionally.

### Phase 2 — Discovery platform

Once the restaurant network reaches critical mass, new revenue lines activate: commission per reservation or transaction processed through the platform, premium plans for restaurants that want more visibility, and potential partnerships with sector brands.
