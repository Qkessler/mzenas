## Audience Variations

### For Restaurant Owner
**Focus on:**
- Revenue increase (table turnover, upsells via chef picks, instant payment)
- Cost savings (fewer order errors, less waiter time on order-taking)
- Data dashboard for business decisions
- Brand control (your colors, your photos)

**Emphasize:**
- "Pagas menos en comisiones que con Uber Eats, y el cliente está en tu local, no en su casa."
- "Tabla promedio sube 12% por upsells automáticos en el carrito."

### For Investor
**Focus on:**
- Market gap (in-restaurant ordering vs delivery apps)
- Scalability (SaaS model, works for tapas bar or Michelin restaurant)
- Network effects (more restaurants = more customer familiarity)
- Metrics: table turnover, average order value, payment conversion

**Emphasize:**
- "We're capturing the €40B European dine-in market, which is 10x larger than delivery."
- "Unit economics: €99/month per restaurant, 40% gross margin, payback in 4 months."

### For Kitchen Staff (Chef/Cook)
**Focus on:**
- KDS clarity (color-coded urgency, no paper tickets)
- Item-level tracking (check off items as they finish)
- Priority system ("Dar prioridad" highlights urgent items)
- No lost orders, no waiter miscommunication

**Emphasize:**
- "No más tickets de papel que se mojan o se pierden."
- "Ves exactamente cuánto tiempo lleva cada pedido, y el sistema te avisa si algo se retrasa."

### For Waiter/Server
**Focus on:**
- Less order-taking (customers do it themselves)
- More time for hospitality (refills, recommendations, table care)
- Table map shows who needs what
- No payment handling (system does it)

**Emphasize:**
- "Tu trabajo se vuelve más fácil y más humano — menos transaccional, más hospitalidad."
- "Las propinas suben porque tienes más tiempo para cuidar a los clientes."
- "No más correr a la cocina para preguntar si algo está listo — lo ves en el mapa de mesas."

## Objection Handling

### "Our customers are older, they won't use an app."
**Response:** It's not an app to download — it's a QR code that opens in their browser. Like opening a website. We've seen 70+ year-olds use it successfully at [pilot restaurant name]. Plus, waiters can still take orders the traditional way for those who prefer it.

### "What if the WiFi goes down?"
**Response:** Linvo caches orders locally and syncs when connection returns. Plus, you can always fall back to traditional service. WiFi reliability is critical for any modern restaurant anyway — credit card machines need it too.

### "We don't want to lose the human touch."
**Response:** Linvo doesn't replace your waiters, it makes them better. Instead of being order-takers, they become hospitality experts — recommending wines, checking on satisfaction, building relationships. The transactional stuff (ordering, payment) happens in the background.

### "How much does it cost?"
**Response:** €99/month for unlimited orders, all features included. No commission per order like delivery apps. Most restaurants break even in week one from increased table turnover alone.

### "What if customers order too much too fast and overwhelm the kitchen?"
**Response:** You can set order pacing in the settings — limit orders per table per 10 minutes, or enable "waiter approval" mode where orders go to a waiter tablet first before kitchen. Totally configurable.

### "Our menu changes daily, is that a problem?"
**Response:** [Show Menu Management screen] You can toggle items on/off in 2 seconds. Many of our restaurants do this for daily specials or when ingredients run out. It updates for all customers instantly.

### "We already use [competitor system]."
**Response:** What do you like about it? What's frustrating? [Listen, then address specific pain points.] Most customers switch to Linvo because of the KDS integration and real-time order status — those are hard to find elsewhere.
